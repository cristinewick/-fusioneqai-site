The Deal That Looked Healthy

This book is for the deal that looked healthy.

Until it didn’t.

The meetings happened.

The customer was engaged.

The next steps were defined.

The forecast absorbed the confidence.

Nothing looked broken.

And that was the problem.

If you have spent enough time in sales, you have probably experienced this. A deal looked strong. Leadership felt confident. The activity supported the story. And somewhere between expectation and outcome, something failed to materialize.

Or perhaps nothing changed at all.

Perhaps the signals were there all along.

They simply meant something different from what we believed.

That experience raises an uncomfortable question:

Why do opportunities that appear healthy disappoint?

The signals were genuine. The interpretation was incomplete.

And interpretation is where outcomes are decided.

A pipeline can tell us what has been recorded. It does not always tell us what those signals mean.

Metrics describe.

Meaning explains.

I came to understand that what I was doing, slowly, across many deals, over many years, was learning to read the decision beneath the deal.

Not the pipeline story.

The decision story.
