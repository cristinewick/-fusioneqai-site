Pattern Briefing

False Momentum

Late-stage opportunity. Forecast steady. Internal ownership unclear.
Visible Signal

Meetings continue. The champion is responsive. Next steps exist. The seller describes the deal as active and on track.

Hidden Pattern

The buyer is cooperating, but not yet carrying the decision. Momentum may be seller-created rather than organization-owned.

Better Move

Stop asking whether the next meeting is scheduled. Test who inside the account is advancing the decision when your team is not present.

The risk is not inactivity. The risk is mistaking responsiveness for ownership.

Leadership Question

Who is accountable inside the buyer organization for turning agreement into action?

Source of the Lens

The thinking behind FusionEQ comes from Cristine Wick's forthcoming book, The Deal Behind the Deal, and a 30-year enterprise sales career spent reading what visible deal activity does not always reveal.

Before enterprise sales, Cristine studied Broadcast News Journalism with a minor in Psychology and spent two years as an intern at KNSD Channel 39. That foundation shaped the interpretive lens behind FusionEQ: visible activity does not always reflect actual decision movement.

Her work now sits at the intersection of emotional intelligence and artificial intelligence, giving leaders a practical way to read alignment, influence, resistance, urgency quality, and decision risk.
