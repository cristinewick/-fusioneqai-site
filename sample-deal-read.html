Deal Snapshot

Stage: Proposal

Deal Size: $1M utilities opportunity

Last Interaction

Customer is engaged, but technical and operational concerns are still being worked through.

Customer Objective

Build confidence in a complex enterprise solution with multiple stakeholders involved.

What Feels Off

  • Multiple stakeholders with different priorities
  • Technical and operational concerns not fully aligned
  • Decision ownership not completely clear

Next Step

Align stakeholders on risk, execution, and decision ownership.

FusionEQ Interpretation
  1. Alignment Risk The deal looks strong, but stakeholder priorities are not fully resolved.
  2. Execution Confidence Gap Technical and operational questions suggest the buying group needs more confidence before approval.
  3. Decision Ownership Gap Unclear ownership can slow a late-stage deal even when conversations are positive.
  4. Pattern Read This does not look like resistance. It looks like risk management.
  5. Recommended Read The deal should be guided toward alignment and execution confidence, not pushed harder toward close.
Executive Summary

This deal is not a simple good deal.

It is a complex deal that requires alignment to close.

Before pushing to close

  • Align the buying group on risk and execution
  • Clarify who owns the decision internally
  • Resolve repeated technical and operational concerns
  • Build confidence across the stakeholder group
