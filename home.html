FAQ

Questions teams usually ask before they bring FusionEQ into live deal work.

What is FusionEQ?
FusionEQ is the interpretation layer for enterprise deal readiness. It reads the context your team provides and identifies what has been evidenced, what remains unproven, and what next move can create clarity.
What is the Deal Readiness Report process?
The report process starts with a guided FusionEQ review of a live deal, presentation, forecast question, or recurring pipeline pattern. It is how FusionEQ gathers the context needed to produce a useful read.
What is a FusionEQ Deal Readiness Report?
The Deal Readiness Report is the polished customer-facing read from the guided FusionEQ review. It includes the readiness score, what has been evidenced, what remains unproven, the identified pattern, forecast confidence read, and Recommended Next Move.
What information do we need to provide?
Start with the information your sales professional or team already has: deal notes, meeting summaries, emails, call observations, stakeholder context, presentation feedback, forecast assumptions, and any known decision criteria.
Do we need to share customer names?
No. FusionEQ can work from anonymized or generalized deal context. Customer names and sensitive account details are not required for an initial Deal Readiness Report conversation.
Does FusionEQ replace our CRM or sales process?
No. FusionEQ does not replace judgment, sales methodology, CRM, or forecast process. It strengthens judgment by helping teams interpret what visible activity actually means.
Who should use FusionEQ?
FusionEQ is designed for enterprise sales professionals, sales managers, enablement leaders, CROs, and deal teams who need a clearer read on decision readiness before the outcome is obvious.
What happens after we submit a request?
FusionEQ reviews the request, clarifies fit and scope, and identifies the right next step: a Deal Readiness Report, Foundations, LENS, READ, CLEAR, or a focused consultation.
