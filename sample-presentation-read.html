Sample FusionEQ Presentation Readiness Report

The report evaluates the presentation from the audience's perspective and tests whether the message can create decision movement, not whether the deck is polished.

Presentation Context

Decision Group

Executive buying group, technical stakeholders, and one internal sponsor.

Intended Decision Move

Create commitment to the recommendation, clarify strategic fit, and move the group toward a defined next-step decision.

Presentation Conditions

The storyline is clear, but the decision path becomes less explicit as the meeting progresses.

What Looks Strong

  • The group stays engaged throughout the conversation
  • Questions and discussion continue after the recommendation is shared
  • Stakeholders appear interested in the topic and the proposed direction

Decision Risk

The conversation does not convert interest into ownership, urgency, or a defined next step.

FusionEQ Interpretation
  1. Visible Read The presentation appears strong: engaged stakeholders, active discussion, and positive response.
  2. Decision Read The presentation did not assign ownership, create urgency, or define the decision path.
  3. What Did Not Change The recommendation was discussed, but no stakeholder became responsible for advancing it.
  4. Signal Interpretation This is not a slide problem. It is a gap between engagement and decision movement.
  5. Hidden Breakpoint The key commitment moment passed without a decision owner, milestone, or pressure test.
  6. Decision Moment The team needed to convert discussion into commitment before leaving the room.
  7. Recommended Adjustments Force a decision point, assign ownership, surface objections, and anchor the next step to a decision milestone.
  8. Confidence Level Medium. The presentation created activity, but decision movement remains unproven.
Executive Summary

The presentation was engaging, but it did not move the decision.

The issue is not effort or polish. It is the absence of ownership, urgency, and a defined next step.

Before the next presentation

  • Name the decision point before the discussion expands
  • Identify who owns the decision after the presentation
  • Surface the objection that could stop movement later
  • Anchor the next step to a decision milestone
