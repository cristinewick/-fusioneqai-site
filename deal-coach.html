Pattern System

Where your deals go off track without looking wrong.

Most revenue organizations already have visibility into their pipeline. The Lens helps identify when those visible signals are being misread.

False Momentum Activity does not equal progress.

The deal shows activity, but nothing is changing the outcome.

  • Meetings are happening without advancing a decision.
  • Next steps are scheduled but not tied to ownership.
  • Stakeholders are engaged but not aligned.

Consequence: This deal may continue to move without getting closer to close.

Next move: Validate whether the next step changes the decision or only extends the conversation.

Ghost Champion Engaged contact. No authority.

The contact is supportive, but not carrying the decision internally.

  • The champion agrees but does not mobilize others.
  • Internal influence is assumed, not proven.
  • The real decision owner remains unclear.

Consequence: The team may overvalue support that cannot move the deal.

Next move: Test what the champion has actually done inside the buying group.

Hidden Stakeholder Decision maker is not actually involved.

The visible group is active, but the person who can decide is absent.

  • Feedback comes from evaluators, not owners.
  • Budget authority is referenced but not present.
  • Decision criteria shift without explanation.

Consequence: The deal can appear aligned while the real decision forms elsewhere.

Next move: Map who owns the decision and require direct alignment before advancing.

Process Illusion Steps are happening. No decision path exists.

The process is busy, but not connected to a real decision sequence.

  • Next steps are procedural instead of decisive.
  • No one can explain the order of approvals.
  • Procurement or legal appears late and undefined.

Consequence: The deal can drift into delay while everyone believes it is progressing.

Next move: Define the decision path, owners, sequence, and risk points.

No Ownership Everyone is involved. No one is responsible.

The buying group is broad, but no one is accountable for moving the decision.

  • Multiple stakeholders participate without a clear owner.
  • Follow-through depends on group consensus.
  • Risks surface indirectly or too late.

Consequence: Momentum depends on the seller pushing instead of the buyer deciding.

Next move: Establish ownership for the decision, timeline, and internal next step.
