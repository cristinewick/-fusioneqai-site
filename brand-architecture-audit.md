# FusionEQ Brand Architecture Audit

Date: May 13, 2026

This note captures the current brand naming decision so it can be reused across chats and future website edits.

## Core Rule

Use `FusionEQ` when describing the category, system, methodology, platform, interpretation layer, or business philosophy.

Use `FusionEQ AI` when specifically referring to the technology engine, AI-powered capabilities, product infrastructure, or formal company identity where the AI component matters.

## Recommended Brand Architecture

### Primary Brand

`FusionEQ`

Use for:

- homepage headlines
- enterprise positioning
- thought leadership
- the Lens
- decision readiness
- executive messaging
- category creation
- training and education
- book references
- methodology discussions

Examples:

- `FusionEQ reads decision readiness.`
- `FusionEQ interprets organizational movement.`
- `The FusionEQ Lens`
- `FusionEQ Deal Analyzer`
- `FusionEQ Enterprise Decision Dynamics`

### Secondary / Formal Company Reference

`FusionEQ AI`

Use for:

- formal company references
- specific AI-enabled system explanations
- technical descriptions
- legal/trademark copy, if needed
- places where the AI component genuinely matters

Examples:

- `FusionEQ AI is the technology engine behind FusionEQ.`
- `FusionEQ AI combines human judgment with AI-assisted interpretation.`
- `Powered by FusionEQ AI.`

### Internal Framework

`FUSION`

Use for:

- the internal framework inside FusionEQ
- framework education
- book/manuscript discussions of the model

## Important Naming Decision

`Lens` works best as the metaphor and system-level idea.

It is less clear as the product name for an individual offer.

### Recommended Structure

- `FusionEQ Lens` = the overarching interpretive lens / way of seeing
- `FusionEQ Deal Analyzer` = the analysis function for deals
- `FusionEQ Deal Coach` = the coaching function for deals
- `FusionEQ Presentation Analyzer` = the analysis function for presentations
- `FusionEQ Presentation Coach` = the coaching function for presentations

## What To Avoid

Avoid using `Deal Lens` and `Presentation Lens` as the primary standalone product names.

Reason:

- they sound elegant, but not always clear
- they read more like perspectives than offers
- cold visitors may not know whether they are tools, reports, coaching, or frameworks

Better usage:

- `Use the FusionEQ Lens to analyze and coach a deal.`
- `Use the FusionEQ Lens to analyze and strengthen a presentation.`

## Website Naming Recommendation

### Keep

- `FusionEQ`
- `FusionEQ Lens`
- `FUSION`

### Shift Product Naming Toward

- `FusionEQ Deal Analyzer`
- `FusionEQ Deal Coach`
- `FusionEQ Presentation Analyzer`
- `FusionEQ Presentation Coach`

Suggested pattern:

### Medium Priority

Current:

Recommended:

or

Choose one based on whether the page should feel more brand-led or company-led.

#### Header/logo alt text

Many live pages still use:

- `alt="FusionEQ AI"`

If the visible brand is now `FusionEQ`, consider updating the alt text to match the displayed brand unless the artwork itself literally reads `FusionEQ AI`.

### Footer / Legal Copy

Current live footer copy:

- `FusionEQAI™ is a trademark of Cristine Wick.`

This needs a deliberate legal/display decision.

Recommended options:

1. If the legal mark must stay exactly as-is:
   - keep footer legal line unchanged
   - use `FusionEQ` everywhere else in public-facing copy

2. If legal allows a cleaner external display:
   - update to a more readable trademark line

Do not change this casually without confirming the intended legal naming.

## Simple Operating Rule For Future Edits

When writing copy:

- If it sounds like a philosophy, category, or enterprise point of view, use `FusionEQ`.
- If it sounds like a tool, engine, or technical layer, consider whether `FusionEQ AI` is actually needed.
- If it is a productized action, prefer `Analyzer` or `Coach`.
- Keep `Lens` as the metaphor for how FusionEQ sees.
